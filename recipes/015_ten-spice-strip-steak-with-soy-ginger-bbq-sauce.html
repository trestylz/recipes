Ten-Spice Strip Steak with Soy-Ginger BBQ Sauce

Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • The Steak:
  • 1 ½ tablespoons five-spice powder
  • 1 ½ tablespoons Creole Seasoning
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 strip steaks (10 to 12 ounces each)
  • The Sauce:
  • 1 tablespoon peanut oil
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh ginger
  • 1 tablespoon chopped garlic
  • Pinch each of kosher salt and black pepper
  • ¾ cup Mutha Sauce
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • ¼ cup water
  • 1 teaspoon Sriracha Hot Chili Sauce (optional)
  • ¼ teaspoon sesame oil
  • Juice of ⅛ lemon
  • ½ cup sliced scallion

Instructions

  1. Throw the sauce together when you get home. Set a saucepan on the stove and turn the heat to medium. Heat up the peanut oil and cook the ginger and garlic with a pinch of salt and pepper for 2 minutes. Add the remaining ingredients, saving 2 tablespoons of the scallions for garnish, and stir to blend. Keep warm.
  2. Fire up the grill. Scrape off the seasoning paste where it’s heavy on the steaks, leaving a light, even coating. Cook the steaks to the desired temperature (see Touch or Temp ). Ladle the sauce over the steaks, and toss on those scallions you saved, to give the whole dish a little color.
