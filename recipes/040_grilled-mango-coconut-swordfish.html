Grilled Mango-Coconut Swordfish

Servings: 5 TO 6

Ingredients

  • The Sauce:
  • 2 mangoes
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and minced
  • 1 tablespoon minced fresh ginger
  • Pinch plus 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • Pinch of black pepper
  • ¾ cup freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 1 can (14 ounces) unsweetened coconut milk ( see note )
  • 3 tablespoons Mutha Sauce
  • 1 teaspoon grated orange zest
  • 1 tablespoon finely diced red bell pepper
  • Juice of ¼ lime
  • Tabasco sauce
  • The Fish:
  • 1 whole loin of swordfish (2 ¾ pounds)
  • Olive oil
  • Creole Seasoning
  • The Garnish:
  • 10 fresh basil leaves

Instructions

  1. Wash the basil leaves, dry them, and stack them. Roll the leaves tightly like a good cigar, and cut across the roll into thin ribbons. Set aside.
  2. Fire up the grill. Cut the skin from the swordfish, and cut the fish in half lengthwise along the grain. Flip each half onto its side and cut into ½-inch-thick steaks across the grain. Rub the steaks down with oil and season with a sprinkling of Creole Seasoning on both sides.
  3. Clean the grill rack and oil it. Spread out a nice hot coal bed. Position the rack and throw the steaks onto it, directly over the coals. Cook for about 2 minutes and flip. Cook for another 1 to 3 minutes or so, til done but not overcooked. These babies should be succulent.
  4. Arrange the steaks on a platter and spoon on the sauce. Dress them up with a sprinkling of basil.
